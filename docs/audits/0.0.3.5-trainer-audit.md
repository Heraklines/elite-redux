# ER Trainer Audit — do Elite/Hell field the right ROM trainers?

## TL;DR

ER's data has the right trainers, but the **marquee ones never appear in their
role**. Gym Leaders, Elite Four, Champions, and the evil-team *leaders* are
registered under PokeRogue trainer-type ids that the game's wave schedule
**never spawns**, so they only ever leak in at random waves (via the
global-unused fallback), not as boss encounters at boss waves.

## The ROM data (vendor/elite-redux/v2.65beta.json)

ER ships distinct important trainer classes:

| ROM class | # trainers | → PokeRogue type |
|---|---|---|
| Leader (gym leaders) | 43 | **200** |
| Elite Four | 16 | **300** |
| Champion | 7 | **350** |
| Monotype Champion | 18 | **350** |
| Johto Champ | 1 | **352** |
| Team Aqua + Aqua Admin | 30 | 72 |
| Team Magma + Magma Admin | 37 | 69 |
| Aqua Leader | 3 | **104** |
| Magma Leader | 3 | **102** |

(Mapping is `ER_ID_MAP.trainerClasses[romClass]` in er-id-map.ts.)

## What the game actually spawns (full 200-wave audit, all difficulties)

PokeRogue trainer types that appeared across a run:

```
63   ×75   (generic "Pokémon Trainer" — the workhorse)
375-378     (the rival stages, waves 8/25/55/95/145/195)
72   ×6    (Team Aqua grunts — appear ✓)
69   ×3    (Team Magma grunts — appear ✓)
70 73 74 82 86 92 93 94 99   (boss/gym-flavored types at the *0/*5 boss waves)
```

**Types 200, 300, 350, 352, 102, 104 NEVER appear.** So:

- The **43 gym Leaders, 16 Elite Four, 25 Champions** and the **Aqua/Magma
  *leaders*** are orphaned — the wave schedule never asks for their type.
- The evil-team **grunts** (types 72/69) *do* appear, so Team Aqua/Magma show up
  — but never their bosses (Archie/Maxie-equivalents at 104/102).
- The endgame "champion" the player fights at w195 is PokeRogue's rival_6
  (type 380), **not** an ER Champion (type 350).

## Why (mechanism)

`getErTrainerForTrainer()` calls `findErTrainersForType(trainer.config.trainerType)`
with the type the GAME chose for that wave. The game chooses from PokeRogue's own
wave/biome trainer pools + fixed rival battles — which use types like 63 / 70-110
/ 375-380, never 200/300/350. So `findErTrainersForType(200…)` is never invoked
by a wave; ER's leaders/E4/champs sit unused. (The new global-unused fallback can
now surface them at a random wave, but stripped of their boss identity and level
placement.)

## Randomness (the other half of the report) — GOOD

With the global-unused pool fix, a single run fields a *different* ER team almost
every trainer battle (Elite: 77 distinct ER rosters / 77 picks; Hell: 96/96), and
the per-run no-repeat is persisted across save/load. So run-to-run and within-run
variety is solid; the gap is purely role placement of the marquee trainers.

## Proposed fix (NOT yet implemented — audit only)

Route the boss/gym/E4/champion/evil-leader WAVES to ER's matching pools:
- At a PokeRogue gym-leader boss wave, pull from ER type 200 (Leaders); at the
  Elite-Four/Champion fixed battles, pull from 300/350; map the evil-team boss
  encounters to 102/104. Keep their teams scaled by the wave (weakest→strongest)
  exactly like the generic pool, so a gym leader at wave 20 is an early-game ER
  gym leader and the champion-tier ones land late.
- This makes the ROM's hand-built gym/E4/champion teams appear *as* bosses,
  instead of random generic ER teams at boss waves, without changing the
  PokeRogue trainer *sprite/identity* the player expects.
