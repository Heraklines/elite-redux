# Elite Redux 0.0.3.5

## 🐛 Bug Fixes

- **Mega Evolution is fixed across the board.** Most ER megas — and **every "Redux" custom mega** (Chandelure Redux, Kleavor Redux, Vanilluxe Redux, and dozens more) — simply had **no mega form wired up**, so they could never appear and players holding the stone **couldn't Mega-Evolve**. We rebuilt mega-form setup from ER's actual evolution data, taking the working count from **186 to 273** of the 283 mega-capable species (the handful left are special non-mega forms like Crowned/Ultra). On top of that, the game **wasn't recognizing most Mega Stones** in trainer hands (it only knew a small slice of the stone list) and would pick the wrong split form — so Mega-X/Mega-Y, Primal, and Origin forms now resolve correctly (Feraligatr Mega X, Primal Dialga/Palkia, etc.). Net effect: far more megas show up where they should, and your own mons can actually Mega-Evolve.

- **No more megas appearing too early in Ace/Elite.** Megas are an end-game power spike and are gated to wave 50+ in Ace and Elite (Hell is intentionally exempt). A few rival/boss aces that are fielded *directly* as a mega (e.g. "Blaziken Mega") slipped past that gate; they now correctly show up in their base form before wave 50.

- **Gallantry no longer acts like Sturdy.** It was wired to the "survive with 1 HP" endure effect (which is what you saw against Iron Valiant). It now does what it should — **negates the first instance of damage** the holder takes (the hit connects and side-effects still apply, but damage is 0).

- **Wyrm Wind no longer stacks its stat change per hit.** It's ER's multi-hit Scale Shot variant: it should raise the user's **Speed +1** and lower the user's **Sp. Def −1** *once*, after all hits. It was applying that on **every** strike (so a 2-hit roll gave +2/−2, a 5-hit roll +5/−5), and the Sp. Def drop was even hitting the wrong target. Now it's a single Speed-up / Sp. Def-down at the end of the move, exactly like Scale Shot (relevant for Mega Latios/Latias).

- **Redux-form icons at the top of the battle screen are correct again.** The small enemy/party icons for Redux forms could show the wrong Pokémon (e.g. a Mega Charizard) because the form's icon frame doesn't exist in the icon sheet and the fallback was broken. They now fall back to the correct base-form icon.

- **Morpeko (Hangry) cleanup.**
  - The **Hangry form no longer shows up in eggs or starter selection** — you'll always get the proper base Morpeko (this also keeps with PokeRogue's rule that no mid/final-stage Pokémon appear in starter selection).
  - Fixed the console spam **"No passive ability found … using run away"** — the Hangry custom (and a couple of its relatives) now report their real passive (Two-Faced / Hunger Switch) instead of falling back to Run Away.
  - **Two-Faced no longer pops up every single turn doing nothing.** The passive's form-switch check was mis-firing each turn on the single-form custom; it now only triggers when it actually has something to do.

- **Infernape (Redux) fixes.**
  - **Correct sprite** — Redux Infernape now shows its **own blue Water/Fighting Redux art**. It had been pinned to the vanilla Infernape sprite after its custom art was mistakenly judged "wrong" — but that art is actually correct, so the override is removed.
  - **Removed the junk "Infernape Redux B"** — a broken placeholder species with no stats or abilities was obtainable; it's now filtered out entirely (along with any other all-blank stub).
  - **Long Reach no longer gets you burned.** Infernape Redux has Long Reach (its contact moves shouldn't make contact), but an internal mis-classification treated a Long-Reach-suppressed contact move as a *ranged* move and let Flame Body's non-contact burn proc anyway. Long Reach now correctly avoids both — no more unexpected burns. (Same class of fix as the earlier "non-contact moves triggering contact abilities" bug.)

- **Catastrophe now actually boosts.** In sun, Water moves (and in rain, Fire moves) should hit *as if* in their favorable weather. It was applying the ×1.5 but not cancelling the adverse-weather penalty, so a Fire move in rain still netted ×0.75 — weaker, not stronger (that's why a Dodrio survived Eruption in rain). It now cancels the penalty and applies the boost (net ×1.5), like Hydro Steam.

- **Flame Body / Static / Poison Point no longer proc off the wrong things.** They were triggering on **status moves** (e.g. an opponent's Leer) and on non-contact moves they shouldn't — which is why people got "burned before doing anything." Now the non-contact tier only fires on an actual damaging attack, and **Poison Point is contact-only** (its bad non-contact tier is removed; Static and Flame Body keep their intended non-contact chance). Switch-in never triggers them.

- **Solar Power no longer chips your HP in sun.** In ER, Solar Power only boosts the holder's highest attacking stat in harsh sunlight — it should not drain 1/8 HP each turn like the vanilla ability. The HP drain was slipping through; now removed (the Sp. Atk boost stays).

- **Calyrex's Ice Rider / Shadow Rider forms now have their innates.** The rider forms were showing their main ability but no passives (innates) because their ER form data wasn't being matched; both riders now get their proper innate triple.

- **Challenge Favour no longer farms extra candy from eggs.** The challenge Favour bonus (shiny + candy, your reward for handicapping a run) was also being applied to candy from eggs you hatch during the run — so a backlog of eggs could farm triple candy under a trivial high-Favour challenge. Egg-hatch candy now keeps only the standard boost, not the run's Favour multiplier. (Egg *shininess* was already unaffected — eggs roll shiny on their own fixed odds — so nothing changes there; we added a test to keep it that way.)

- **Mega Evolution note:** the broad Mega fix above also restores the **Redux-custom megas** that previously "didn't work" (e.g. Mega Redux Reuniclus) — they now have their forms wired. And to be clear: **Ace mode does have megas** — they show up on the late-game gym-leader / Elite-Four / Champion trainers (Mega Kangaskhan, Mega Venusaur, **Mega Rayquaza** on the champion, etc.), just never before wave 50.

- **Every Redux line is now reachable from eggs.** Some lines ship only an *evolved* Redux with no base-form Redux to hatch and evolve up from (e.g. **Weavile Redux** with no Sneasel Redux; **Flygon Redux B** with no Vibrava/Trapinch Redux B). Those were unobtainable. Now, when a line has no lower Redux to evolve from, the **lowest existing Redux form hatches directly** — at a higher egg/starter cost reflecting its evolution stage. (Lines that *do* have a base Redux are unchanged: you still hatch the base and evolve up. And note: there is no "Sneasel Redux" in ER's data — only Weavile Redux — so the Sneasel line is obtained via Weavile Redux directly.)

- **Redux Pokémon no longer evolve into a Gigantamax/Mega form.** A Redux-form mon (e.g. Krabby) could evolve into a battle-only form of its evolution (G-max Kingler) when the pre/post form layouts differed — which also caused an evolution-screen crash. Evolutions now never land on a Mega/Primal/Gigantamax/Eternamax form; they use the normal form.

- **Battle Bond is now generalized** (was hard-coded to Greninja), so ER Battle-Bond builds can transform on a KO. (Darmanitan Redux Bond → Blunder still needs its forms wired before it's reachable in-game — tracked.)

- **Verified working (reported, not actually bugs):** V-create *does* lower the user's Defense/Sp.Def/Speed on hit — the report was a 95%-accuracy **miss** (no on-hit effects fire on a miss). And a Rock move hitting a Flying-type for 2× under **Gravity** is correct — Gravity only removes a Flying-type's *Ground* immunity; it never changes other type matchups. (Regression tests added for both.)

- **Gym Leaders, Elite Four, Champions & evil-team Leaders now appear as bosses (Elite/Hell).** ER's hand-built marquee teams (43 gym leaders, 16 Elite Four, 25 champions, the Aqua/Magma leaders) were mapped to trainer types the wave schedule never picked, so they never showed up in their boss role — boss waves got generic teams. Now boss waves pull from ER's marquee pool, wave-scaled (early gym-leader-tier early, champion-tier at the end), so e.g. an Elite Four wave fields a real ER Elite Four / champion team and a mid-game gym boss fields an ER gym-leader team — all still different every battle and every run. (Rivals keep their own path.)

- **Far more trainer variety in Elite/Hell.** Even with the no-repeat system, a run was only fielding ~19–20 *distinct* ER trainer teams (some repeated 7–8 times), because once a given trainer *type's* small pool was used up it would start repeating that type. Now, once a type's fresh trainers run out, the game pulls from the **whole** unused ER pool (400+ tier-eligible trainers) before ever repeating — so a full run now fields a different ER team essentially every trainer battle (Elite: 77 distinct; Hell: 96 distinct). Strength still scales with the wave, so early fights stay easy and Elite-Four/Champion-tier teams still show up late.

- **Elite/Hell trainers no longer repeat after a reload.** The system that stops a difficulty from fielding the same trainer twice tracked "already fought" trainers **only in memory** — and explicitly **wiped that memory every time a run was loaded/resumed**. So reloading or continuing a Hell run reset the pool and re-fought the same (early/weakest) trainers — which is why waves 5 and 15 could be the same battle and you kept seeing the same teams. The used-trainer list is now **saved with your run and restored on load**, so the no-repeat guarantee survives save/load. *(Older saves restore to a fresh pool — no migration needed.)*

- **Post-battle freeze fixed (eggs).** A leftover/blank ER species id could slip into the egg pool; when a freshly-restocked egg happened to roll a rare/epic *variant*, the hatch code dereferenced that non-existent species and **froze the game** right after a wild battle ended (the `EggLapse` hang). The egg pool now refuses any species that isn't actually registered, with a second safety net in the hatch roll so no single bad id can ever lock up a hatch again.

- **"Wooly Worm" is now an Epic-tier egg.** (Was banding as Rare by stats.)

- **Darmanitan Redux "Bond"/"Blunder" no longer hatch or appear as starters.** These are battle-only Battle-Bond forms (like Mega/Primal/Hangry), not base mons, so they were wrongly showing up fully-evolved in egg hatches and the starter list. They're now excluded the same way the other battle-only forms are.

- **No Turning Back no longer boosts over and over.** The "+1 to all stats below ½ HP" was re-triggering every time HP dropped back under half — so a Sitrus Berry (heal above ½, then get hit below ½ again) handed out *another* full boost. It now fires exactly **once per battle**, sharing the same guard as its no-switch trap.

- **Effect Spore & Poison Touch are contact-only again.** Like the earlier Poison Point fix, these had a stray "non-contact" tier that let *ranged* moves trigger them. Removed — they only proc on contact now (Poison Touch keeps its on-contact offense proc).

- **Purple Haze (Redux Scizor) fires right after the attack.** Its follow-up Poison Gas was being re-sorted into turn speed-order (so it could resolve much later); it now resolves immediately after the move that triggered it, at its intended 20 BP.

- **Devious Shot now actually bleeds.** It applied the Bleed status but with no duration, so it never ticked (unlike Blood Shot). It now inflicts the proper ticking Bleed (4–6 turns), plus its +1 crit stage.

- **Poison vs Flying respects the type chart.** ER's "Poison is super-effective vs Flying" (Poison Gas) was *forcing* a flat 2× and overriding everything else — so a Ground/Flying mon like Gligar took 2× even though Ground resists Poison. Now the Flying part is super-effective and **multiplies into the rest of the chart**: pure Flying = 2×, Ground/Flying = 1× (0.5 × 2), Steel/Flying = 0× (Steel's immunity stays).

- **Dragon Rage deals real damage.** It was stuck on vanilla's fixed-40-damage behavior; it's now ER's normal 80-BP Dragon attack (full stats/STAB/type-chart/crits apply).

- **Pokédex luck no longer lies for ER customs.** An *uncaught* custom (e.g. Kipmodo) was showing "Luck: 3" because the always-inspectable Pokédex used a fully-unlocked stand-in mask to read luck. Luck now comes from your real caught data (0 / hidden until you've actually caught one).

- **Locust Swarm / Hivemind direction fixed.** It was inverted — the holder went *into* Hivemind form when it dropped to ¼ HP. Per "Hivemind form until ¼ HP or less," it's now the other way round (Wishiwashi-style): **Hivemind while above ¼ HP, reverting to base at ¼ or below.** (Ape Shift's genuine low-HP transform is unchanged.)

- **Locust Swarm / Hivemind now works — and shows the right sprite.** Wispywaspy's "Hivemind" form is injected, so the ability swaps into it at ≤¼ HP and reverts once you heal back above it (two-way, not stuck). Crucially, the injected form (and the base form too) now render the correct ER art instead of 404ing: injecting any form onto an ER-custom species made the game resolve sprites via a path that doesn't exist for custom mons — the same bug that hit Unown Revelation — so both the base and the alternate form are now redirected to their real ER sprites/icons.

- **Darmanitan Redux Battle Bond now transforms — with correct sprites.** The "Blunder" battle form is wired onto the Bond species with a Battle-Bond trigger, so a KO promotes Bond → Blunder one-way (like Greninja → Ash-Greninja), and both the Bond base form and the Blunder form now render their real ER art (same custom-form sprite fix as Wispywaspy above). The fully-evolved Bond/Blunder forms still don't hatch or appear as starters — see the note below on reaching Bond in the first place.

- **More multi-hit / move-power corrections.** **Thunder Shock** now correctly hits at **80 BP** (a stale internal override pinned it to 40). **Cross Poison** was doing **90 BP per hit on a 2-hit move** (180 total) — corrected to ER's **40 BP/hit** (its 2-hit / high-crit / 10% poison are unchanged).

- **Chilling Water inflicts Frostbite, not an Attack drop.** ER's Chilling Water is a 75-BP Water move with a **30% chance to inflict Frostbite**, but it shares the vanilla move's name and was being pinned to the vanilla effect (a guaranteed Attack drop). It now drops the Attack debuff and rolls the 30% Frostbite instead.

- **Every ER Mega (and Redux-custom form) shows its real sprite — and no longer freezes the fight.** Megas that ER adds to Pokémon with **no** vanilla Mega (e.g. **Mega Goodra**) **and all Redux-custom Megas** (e.g. **Mega Mawile Redux B**) — plus the base form of any Redux-custom that has a Mega — were resolving their sprite to a vanilla path that doesn't exist (`6706-mega`, `10773-mega`, etc.) → the art 404'd, showed a "substitute" placeholder, and on the live build could **stall the loader and freeze the battle** (repro: Mega Goodra in a Veteran fight). All of these now point at their actual ER art (`elite-redux/{slug}/…`) — both the auto-injected vanilla-base Megas and the data-driven Redux-custom Megas (and their seeded base forms) — so they render correctly and don't hang. (Same fix family as the Hivemind/Blunder/Unown sprite redirects.)

- **Elite/Hell trainers now rotate across runs (no more identical cast).** The run seed *was* changing every run, but each trainer slot only picked from its tiny per-type pool (often 1–2 trainers for Youngster/Lass/…), so two different runs kept fielding the **same team at the same wave**. Regular waves now draw from the **whole difficulty pool** (hundreds of trainers), strength-windowed to stay wave-appropriate, so every run gets a genuinely different cast — while still never repeating a trainer within a single run. (Boss waves keep their hand-built gym-leader/Elite-Four/champion teams.)

- **Freeze is gone — it's Frostbite now, everywhere.** The vanilla **FREEZE** status was still being applied (e.g. wild mons showing "FRZ") instead of ER's **Frostbite**. Every freeze source — vanilla Ice moves, abilities, anything — now inflicts Frostbite instead, with Frostbite's own rules (Ice-types are immune). You'll never see "FRZ" again.

- **Multi-Headed now hits once per head — not always 3×.** The ability was hardcoded to strike **3 times for every holder**, so two-headed mons (Doduo, Mawile, Weezing, Girafarig, Vanilluxe, Klink, Zweilous, Binacle) wrongly hit thrice. Head counts now come straight from the ER ROM's own two-/three-headed species flags: **two-headed mons strike twice, three-headed** (Dugtrio, Magneton/Magnezone, Dodrio, Exeggutor, Combee, Probopass, Klang/Klinklang, Hydreigon, Barbaracle — plus Mega Mawile and Mega Shuckle, which grow a third head) **strike three times.** (Later heads already deal reduced damage.)

- **ER statuses are now cured by everything that should cure them.** Frostbite, Bleed and Fear are custom statuses the game didn't treat as "real" status conditions, so cures ignored them. Now **Lum Berry, Full Heal/Full Restore, Heal Bell/Aromatherapy, Rest**, and the status-clearing abilities **Natural Cure, Shed Skin, Hydration, and Healer** all clear them, just like burns/poison/etc. (Frostbite's gradual self-cure is its intended duration.)

- **Aftermath explodes *before* the holder can sneak in another move.** When a faster foe KO'd an Aftermath holder, the holder could still resolve its own queued move before exploding; the explosion now fires immediately on faint.

- **Thundercall triggers off Thunder Shock again.** A re-entry guard was swallowing the holder's own Thunder Shock (since Thundercall's follow-up *is* Thunder Shock); fixed so it triggers properly (and this fix also helps the other follow-up-move abilities).

- **Pokédex candy costs match the real (reworked) prices.** The Pokédex's "buy candy upgrades" menu still showed the **old** passive-unlock costs (the steep ×1/×2/×4 scheme) while the starter screen had moved to the cheaper reworked prices — so unlocking a passive from the Pokédex looked far more expensive. Both screens now share one cost formula, so they always agree.

- **Double battles show both foes' move effectiveness.** When re-opening the fight menu, the second foe's effectiveness indicator stayed hidden until the first foe fainted; both are shown now while choosing a move.

- **More Epic-tier eggs.** Tornadus (Therian) and the Typhlosion custom "Lumbering Sloth Engulfed" are now Epic-tier, matching their legendary/regional pedigree.

- **Ability Randomizer now works on fused Pokémon.** It writes the active-ability override, but a fused mon's ability is resolved on a separate path that ignored that override — so the reroll did nothing. Fused mons now honor the override (and the reroll takes effect).

- **ER-custom sprites with a baked-in background are cleaned up.** A few ER sprites (e.g. Redux Noibat) shipped without transparency, so the mon showed inside a coloured box. The background is now keyed out transparently when the sprite loads (conservatively — only when it's confident there's a flat background, so correctly-drawn sprites are untouched).

- **Many-form families no longer flood egg hatches.** Families that exist as lots of separate forms — Unown's 28 letters, Arceus's 17 type plates, Silvally's 17, the 15 Pikachu caps, Furfrou's trims, Castform/Rotom, etc. — were each appearing individually, so collectively they dominated the pool. Each form's hatch weight is now divided by its family size, so the whole family shows up at roughly the rate of a single Pokémon (you'll still see them, just not constantly).

- **Unown's "Revelation" Schooling works end-to-end.** Unown schools into its Revelation form on entry while above ¼ HP, reverts to a normal letter once it drops to ¼ HP or below, and reverts on faint so it never persists stuck in Revelation (its stored/party form returns to a normal Unown). The schooled form now shows the **correct Revelation sprite** (it was falling back to a broken placeholder because the art lives under the ER-custom sprite set), the **correct main abilities** (Anticipation / Minion Control / Magic Bounce), and the **correct innates** (Revelation / Wonder Skin / Unown Power) — all per-form, so base Unown letters keep their own. The mechanic is its signature innate, so like other form-change innates (Forecast, Hunger Switch, …) it's exempt from the passive-unlock gate and always active.

- **Redux Kakuna → Mega Beedrill (already fixed):** evolving onto a held-stone Mega/Gigantamax form is blocked in the current build — the report was from the older live build and resolves on this deploy.

## ✨ New Features

- **🎁 Free gift: 2 Legendary eggs for everyone (one-time).** Every player receives **two Legendary-tier eggs**, granted automatically — no action needed; they'll be waiting in your egg list. It's a **one-time** gift: once you've received it, it never triggers again (tracked on your save, so it won't re-grant on reload or on another device). Thanks for playing!

- **Damage Calc panel in the fight menu.** While choosing a move, press **R** (controller: **RB**; mobile: the on-screen **R** button) to cycle the right-hand panel from the move's stats to a **Damage Calc** view. It shows the highlighted move's damage as a **percentage range of the target's current HP** (and flags a **possible/guaranteed KO**), so you can read at a glance how hard each move hits.

  The panel is **locked until you hold a Damage Calculator** (the master-tier item). Until then, cycling to it shows a "Locked — find a Damage Calculator" message so you know it's there; once you pick one up, it unlocks and shows live numbers. Status moves show "—", and it gracefully says "no target" if nothing's on the field.
