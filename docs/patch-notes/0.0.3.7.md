# Elite Redux 0.0.3.7

## Abilities
- **Eerie Fog reworked.** Fog no longer drops accuracy. Instead, at the end of each turn it **saps one stage off every raised stat** of non-Ghost/non-Psychic Pokémon, and **Ghost-types take reduced damage** while it's up. Ghost/Psychic mons keep their boosts. (Ominous Wind also hits for **2× power** in fog — see Moves.)
- **Normalize now ignores resistances.** A Normalize move that's normally resisted (e.g. Tackle vs Steelix) now lands for **neutral** damage. Immunities still apply — a Normal move still does nothing to a Ghost.
- **Gifted Mind nulls the holder's Psychic weakness.** A Fighting-type with Gifted Mind (and Galarian Articuno's "Null Psychic Weakness") now takes **neutral** damage from Psychic instead of super-effective.
- **Fire Aspect burns on every hit.** The holder's attacks — even non-Fire ones — now inflict **burn**.
- **Pressure clears the foe's stat boosts on entry.** Switching a Pressure mon in now snaps the opposing Pokémon's **positive** stat stages back to zero.
- **Molten Down no longer over-applies super-effective.** It used to treat *every* Rock-type hit as 2× and ignore the target's other type; damage is now correct (e.g. neutral vs Water/Rock Relicanth).
- **Oricorio styles carry their own abilities.** Pom-Pom Oricorio was fighting with Baile's **Flash Fire** innate; each style now has its proper kit (Pom-Pom = **Lightning Rod**, Pa'u = Psychic Mind, Sensu = Phantom Pain).
- **Multi-Headed: the three-headed customs hit 3×.** Head counts now come straight from the ER data for every custom — Sandy Shocks, Pentadug, and Wugtrio were only striking twice despite their three heads.

## Moves
- **Slash and Cut reworked to ER spec.** Both are 60 BP / 100% accurate and **always crit** — Slash at 10 PP with a **20% chance to inflict Bleed**, Cut at 20 PP with a **10% Bleed chance** (Cut stays Steel-type and usable in the field).
- **Mud-Slap reworked**: 25 BP / 100% / 10 PP, now **hits 2-5 times** and no longer drops accuracy.
- **Hidden Power is 80 BP** (it was stuck at 70).
- **Triple Kick is 20 BP** as the dex says (in-game wrongly used 25; it still ramps 20/40/60 across its three kicks).
- **Secret Power is now a physical Hidden Power** — 80 BP, its type varies with the user; the old terrain side-effect is gone.
- **Techno Blast works like Hidden Power** — its type varies with the user (no Drive items) — at 120 BP / 5 PP.
- **Triple Axel (and the other Triple-* moves) hit and ramp correctly** — three strikes, each stronger than the last, instead of fizzling.
- **Fury Cutter and Echoed Voice now work like Triple Kick** — 20 BP / 90% accurate (Fury Cutter 10 PP, Echoed Voice 15 PP), hitting **3 times with rising power** (20/40/60) instead of the vanilla "stronger on consecutive use" ramp.
- **27 custom moves now hit their real targets.** Every ER custom move was quietly single-target — Outburst now hits **every other mon on the field**, the genie Storms / Mortal Spin / Make It Rain hit **both foes**, Raging Fury picks a random foe, and Caltrops/Creeping Thorns lay hazards on the enemy side.
- **The genie Storms set their fields.** Bleakwind Storm now actually **sets Tailwind** on your side (as its description says), Wildbolt Storm sets rain, Sandsear Storm sets sandstorm, and Springtide Storm sets misty terrain — replacing the vanilla stat-drop/status side effects.
- **Aura Force hits Ghosts** — the Fighting-type move now connects with Ghost-types **and** is super-effective (it used to do nothing, like a normal Fighting move into a Ghost).
- **Clay Dart hits Flying-types** — same fix as Aura Force: the Ground-type move now connects with Flying mons **and** is super-effective, instead of bouncing off Ground's immunity.
- **Boiling Flame is boosted by rain** as described — it was wrongly *weaker* in rain (the generic rain penalty on Fire moves was applying on top).
- **Decorate now damages the foe and raises the USER's stats** — it was buffing the wrong side.
- **Berry Smash now eats one of the user's berries**, as its description says — gaining that berry's effect. If you're holding several, it picks one at random. (It dealt damage but ate nothing before.)
- **Ominous Wind deals 2× in fog** (base 60 → 120), matching the Eerie Fog rework.
- **Dragon Rage's move info now reads 80 BP** (it showed "1" before — a leftover from its old fixed-damage version). The damage was already correct; only the displayed number was wrong.
- **Dragon Rage now damages Fairy-types**, as its description says — it hits them **neutrally** instead of bouncing off the usual Dragon-into-Fairy immunity.

## Forms & Evolution
- **Every Mega and Primal is now actually obtainable.** A full audit found **51 megas whose stone never worked or never appeared** — second megas of multi-mega species (Mega Charizard Z, Mega-Y Venusaur/Blastoise/Gengar/Wigglytuff/Lapras, Mega Gyarados X, …), all the regional-form megas (Hisuian Arcanine/Typhlosion/Samurott/Goodra/Decidueye, Galarian Rapidash/Slowbro/Slowking, Alolan Sandslash), per-form megas (Pikachu caps, Oricorio styles, Tatsugiri, Meowstic-F, Magearna Original, Toxtricity Low-Key), **every Redux Mega**, and the Espeon/Eternatus/Apex Primals. All 51 now have their correct, distinct stones (e.g. Lucario: Mega X uses Lucarionite, Mega Z uses Lucarionite Z).
- **Redux lines keep their Redux form through their FINAL evolution.** A Redux-form mon (e.g. Redux Kadabra) now evolves into the **Redux** version of its next stage — with the right sprite, typing, **and its Redux learnset** (so lines that should get Ice moves now do) — instead of reverting to the plain form.
- **Redux lines now reach their TRUE final evolutions.** 15 Redux lines whose last stage is a unique species were cut off from it — and worse, they evolved down the **normal** line instead. Now: Redux Psyduck → **Shyduck** (not plain Golduck), Redux Cinccino → **Frostuccino** (not Beniccino), Redux Excadrill → **Rexcadrill**, Redux Raticate → **Eraticate**, Redux Volcarona → **Selenumbra**, Redux Floatzel → **Velozel**, plus Bewarden, Escarginite, Toxtricity Fuzz, Crabruiser, Magnemous, Bariong, Frostula, Kilozuna, and Terrow lines. And it's strict both ways: a **normal** mon never gets the Redux evolution, and a **Redux** mon never falls down the normal line.

## Sprites & Icons
- **Redux mons show their Redux ICON everywhere** (party, box, summary) — icons were falling back to the base form's icon.
- **Stale/wrong sprites self-heal.** Some players saw mismatched art (e.g. a Psyduck icon showing Mega Charizard X) because the asset CDN kept serving a days-old cached sheet after updates. Assets are now pinned to exact versions, so everyone gets the matching art immediately — no cache clearing needed.
- **The intro video plays on the live site again** (a cross-origin hiccup kept it from loading).

## Rewards & Costs
- **Innate-gated items stay out of the reward pool until you can use them.** Items that only help a *locked* innate (e.g. a Mystical Rock for an un-unlocked Seed Sower) no longer clutter your shop/reward choices.
- **Innate unlock costs use the real candy base.** The cost formula was reading the raw starter-point cost instead of the actual candy base price, over- or under-charging some species.

## Battles & Modes
- **Ace mode is pure vanilla PokeRogue.** No ghost trainers in Ace at all (the ghost gauntlet is now Elite/Hell-only), no ER trainer rosters, the vanilla Eternatus finale, and the early high-power spawn gate applies.
- **Elite mode re-tuned.** Slightly fewer forced trainer battles (every 4th eligible wave instead of every 3rd), a **slower team-strength ramp** (top-end teams now arrive near the end of the run, not by wave ~180), and trainers now draw from the **entire 895-trainer cast** — over half the roster (trainers without a high-difficulty team) could never appear before; their weaker base teams now fill the early and mid game.
- **Battle-Factory teams join the Elite/Hell trainer pool.** Nearly 2,000 competitive ER Battle-Factory sets (species + tuned movesets + ability picks) now appear sporadically — about 1 in 7 regular trainer waves fields a factory team matched to the wave's strength, so the trainer pool is far less repetitive. Ace mode is untouched.
- **Ghost trainers appear throughout the run, with real names.** Ghost battles (real players' teams) used to exist only in the final stretch (wave 176+/192+), so almost nobody met one; mid-run ghost waves now pepper Elite and Hell climbs too. And the ghost trainer now carries the **account name of the player whose team you're fighting**.
- **Ghost trainers can come from any difficulty's runs** — the endgame ghost pool (Elite/Hell) now draws from the whole player base, not just your own tier (they're re-levelled to your wave either way).
- **The final rival battle is a real finale again.** On Elite/Hell the wave-195 rival was fielding a leftover early-game team — unevolved Pokémon, even contest-gimmick sets (Smeargle, Rock-Star Pikachu) from stale data — and no ace. Rival battles now climb the proper stage ladder (Route 103 → Lilycove) with the REAL teams from the ER game data, and the final battle's last Pokémon is always **Mega Rayquaza**, like the classic finale.
- **Ghost battles have their own theme.** Fighting another player's ghost team now plays a dedicated piano track instead of the regular trainer battle music.
- **Primal Cascoon is no longer obtainable.** Its activation orb was sneaking into the reward pool for a party Cascoon; the final-boss form is boss-only again.

## Eggs & Hatching
- **Only the BASE form of a line can hatch (or appear in starter select).** Fully-evolved and battle-form customs (e.g. **Aegislash Blade Redux**) were hatchable; the whole class is excluded now — every line hatches from its lowest form (e.g. Honedge Redux).

## Saves
- **Lost runs can no longer be "continued".** Since the account system, a defeated run's cloud save survived on the server, so Continue loaded an all-fainted party and bounced you to the title in a loop. Cleared runs are now fully deleted server-side; stuck saves self-heal on your next game-over.

## UI & Quality of Life
- **Move descriptions are back while choosing moves.** Pressing **R** in the fight menu now cycles the right panel through **Stats → Description → Damage Calc** — descriptions had become unviewable in battle after the panel redesign. (The R key was also being swallowed before it ever reached the fight menu — the old in-battle damage-calc toggle had never actually worked because of this.)
- **The in-battle move description shows in a proper window** above the move list (it was rendering borderless text on top of the move names), and the **in-battle Damage Calc now counts every hit of multi-hit moves** — Triple Axel and friends were showing a single lowest-power strike.
- **The Damage Calculator now shows your 5th move.** Mons with the rogue-tier extra move slot had their last move cut off the Damage Calculator panel; all five now display.
- **Pause menu no longer overflows on login builds** — Report Bug now lives under Community, making room for Log Out.
- **A lost run can no longer be continued.** Defeats now always finalize, so you can't resume into a broken half-dead run.
- **"Players online" is real.** The title-screen counter shows accounts active in the last few minutes, not the all-time account total.

## Starters
- **Squawkabilly's cosmetic plumage duplicates are gone from the starter grid.** The colour variants showed as separate starters with mismatched costs (5 vs 2); only the real starter remains, at the right price.
