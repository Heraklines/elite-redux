# Elite Redux 0.0.3.8

## Items (community batch, all approved suggestions)
- **Resistance berries and Ward Stones drop about twice as often.** Resist berry rolls are now 5% on Ace, 10% on Elite and 20% on Hell per trainer mon (was 1/5/10). Ward Stone rolls on Elite and Hell are doubled (bosses: Prime 20%, Greater 30%; regular trainer mons: Greater 10%, Minor 40%), and Ace now has a flat 5% Ward Stone chance from wave 150 (it previously never saw them).
- **The challenge screen has a proper Last Setup button.** It sits in the header next to the Challenge Modifiers title (the Favour readout was compacted to make room), appears once you have started at least one challenge run, and pressing UP from the top challenge row focuses it - confirm to re-apply your last configuration in one press. The old hidden L/R-on-Start shortcut and the R hotkey still work.
New rewards in the item pools, straight from your Discord suggestions:
- **Chili Sample** (Ultra): the holder's damaging moves gain a 10% chance to burn, even non-contact ones.
- **Rusty Claw** (Ultra): the holder's contact moves gain a 10% chance to poison.
- **Spiked Knuckles** (Ultra): the holder's contact moves gain a 10% chance to inflict Bleed.
- **Copper Rod** (Ultra): 10% paralysis chance on contact, BOTH ways - when you strike with a contact move, and when an enemy strikes you with one.
- **Loaded Dice** (Ultra, stacks to 3): each stack raises the minimum number of strikes of 2-to-5-hit moves by one. Three stacks means always 5 hits.
- **Lucky Heart** (Ultra, stacks to 2): +15% move effect chance per stack, additive on top of abilities like Serene Grace.
- **Frostbite Orb** (Ultra): the Toxic/Flame Orb sibling - frostbites the holder at the end of each turn, for all your Guts-style shenanigans.
- **Dex Nav** (Ultra): scans the current biome and lets you register any 2 of its wild Pokemon in your Pokedex as caught.
- **Power Herb** (Ultra): skips the charge turn of two-turn moves like Solar Beam. Holds 2 charges and regains one every 10 waves, so it never fully runs out.
- **Charge items show a live counter.** Power Herb and Omni Gem display their remaining charges on the item icon (green number, red when empty), counting down as charges are spent and back up as the herb recharges.
- **Omni Gem** (Rogue): doubles the damage of the holder's next damaging move. It carries 2 charges and shatters once both are spent.
- **Ability Capsule** (Great): switches a Pokemon's active ability to its species' next legal ability (1 to 2 to hidden). Works once per Pokemon - the Ability Randomizer remains the rerollable option.
- **Learner's Shroom** (Ultra): teaches a Pokemon ANY move it is capable of learning - TMs, tutor moves, egg moves (no unlock needed) and level-up moves it has already reached. The one exception: level-up moves it has not reached yet stay locked. Comes with its own move picker.

## Moves (community spec report)
- **Flash now hits BOTH foes.** It is natively multi-target in Elite Redux (60 BP Electric, 50% chance to drop Attack) but was imported as single-target.
- **Move descriptions now show their ability-boost notes.** High Jump Kick reads "Striker boost" again, and the whole family of notes is restored across 85 moves: Striker (kicks), Iron Fist (punches), Strong Jaw (bites), Keen Edge (slicing moves), Mighty Horn and Mega Launcher. The note is appended to the existing description, nothing else changes.
- **NO move has more than 20 PP anymore.** Everything above 20 is clamped to 20, matching the ER dex.
- **20 moves re-pinned to their real ER stats** (stale values had crept in): Razor Wind 70 BP, Slam 95 BP / 10 PP, Vine Whip 80 BP with 30% flinch, Double Kick 45 BP, Double-Edge 130 BP / 10 PP, Acid 70 BP (super effective vs Steel), Ember 20 BP with 100% burn, Strength 110 BP / 5 PP, Mega Drain 50 BP, Fury Swipes 25 BP, Hyper Fang 85 BP, Flame Wheel 40 BP, Rollout 40 BP / 100% acc, all three elemental Fangs 80 BP, Wood Hammer 10 PP, Synchronoise 95 BP, Snarl 60 BP, Steel Roller 80 BP.
- **Brine is 70 power and super effective against Water** (was 65). Acid keeps its 70 power, super effectiveness against Steel, and 30% chance to lower Sp. Def.
- **Razor Wind no longer takes a charge turn** - it hits immediately, is Flying-type, high-crit, and super effective against Rock.
- **Strength now drops the user's defenses** like Close Combat (it is Rock-type, 110 BP, as the dex says).
- **Mega Drain heals 75% of the damage dealt** (was 50%).
- **Flame Wheel is a proper Rollout clone** - rising power each consecutive use, no burn chance.
- **Synchronoise matches the user's SECOND type** and hits any target (it only damaged same-typed targets before).
- **Steel Roller is usable without terrain** and still clears one when present.
- **Springtide Storm no longer fails on misty terrain** - none of the genie Storms fail when their own field is already up; the field-setting is a no-op instead.

## Battles & Modes
- **The Hell finale no longer kills itself at stage 2.** The black shiny Primal Cascoon played its 'reveal my true form' dialogue and then instantly died, because the Hell version starts already in Primal form: the stage-one '1 HP floor' protection only checked for the base form, and the black shiny promotion skipped the heal-and-reshield step the normal form change gets. Both fixed - stage 2 now begins at full HP with 5 fresh shields.
- **Power-ceiling replacements now show the right sprite.** When the Elite wave ceiling swapped a too-strong team member (or an early mega was reverted), the battle kept showing the ORIGINAL Pokemon's sprite under the replacement's name - an Iron Voca posing as 'Swadloon'. The replacement now reloads its own artwork.
- **Elite's early game no longer throws legendaries and final evolutions at you.** Elite trainer teams now respect a wave-based power ceiling: a team member over the ceiling is devolved a stage, and legendaries (banned before wave 80) or heavy single-stage mons are swapped for a wave-appropriate pick. Measured result: wave 1-20 trainer teams dropped from an average 456 BST with box legendaries (Kyogre at wave 20!) to an average 342 BST with none. Boss waves keep a little extra headroom so gym leaders still feel like bosses, waves past 100 are untouched, and Hell keeps its brutal early spikes on purpose.
- **New challenge: Ghost Trainers (7 Favour).** Every Trainer battle is fought against a ghost team - a real team from another player's run (wins and losses both feed the pool), matched to your current wave. Teams are matched within 20 waves of where their run ended; if nothing fits, the search widens step by step, and as a last resort the closest deeper team is taken, with its members devolved (down to base forms for big gaps) and re-leveled to your wave, so an endgame roster arrives as its early-game self instead of sweeping you. The challenge draws from every difficulty's ghost pool, and wins and losses both feed it. You only get a normal trainer if no stored run got as far as your current wave.
- **Ghost challenge pool hardened against startup races.** The pool now fills incrementally as each batch arrives (the very first trainer waves no longer race the network against an empty pool), and if every source comes back empty (login token not ready yet, a network blip) the game retries on the next wave instead of silently giving you normal trainers for the whole run. The whole ghost pipeline also logs its decisions now, so a "normal trainer in ghost mode" report pinpoints the failing stage from the console log.
- **Ghost variety: no more meeting the same player every wave.** The matcher used to deterministically pick the shallowest fitting run, so whichever single player owned the most early-ending runs showed up wave after wave. Picks are now spread across the eligible window, back-to-back waves prefer a DIFFERENT player than the previous ghost, and no single account can fill more than 3 slots of the run's ghost pool.
- **Ghost trainers come in many flavors.** Ghosts used to always appear as a Veteran; they now show up as one of 15 trainer classes (Ace Trainer, Black Belt, Psychic, Hiker, Scientist, Swimmer, Ranger, Hex Maniac and more). The same ghost team always appears as the same class, so you can recognize a returning rival at a glance.
- **Wave 5 is a ghost too now.** The 5th wave is a scripted, fixed Youngster/Lass battle baked into classic mode, and it slipped past the Ghost Trainers hook entirely - which is why, no matter what we fixed, the 5th trainer was ALWAYS a plain Youngster or Lass. In the challenge that battle now fields a ghost like every other trainer wave. Story battles (your rival, the evil teams, the Elite Four and the Champion) intentionally keep their scripted trainers.
- **The Copper Rod looks like a copper rod now.** Its shop and held-item icon was a copper-tinted Quick Claw, which read as "a claw". It is now an actual copper rod crackling with sparks, matching its effect (10% paralysis on contact, both ways).
- **Ogerpon Cornerstone Mask audited clean.** An old report said its Rockhard Will innate was dead and Ivy Cudgel did not change type per mask. Both work (they were fixed by the big innate-system passes along the way) and are now locked in by tests: Ivy Cudgel is Rock on Cornerstone, Water on Wellspring, Fire on Hearthflame, and Rockhard Will boosts Rock moves 1.2x (1.5x below a third HP). The ER learnset is in place too.
- **Power of Alchemy actually transmutes now.** Alolan Muk and Grimer's signature showed the ER text (transmute berries on entry) but still had vanilla's copy-a-fainted-ally effect. On entry it now destroys every Berry the opposing side is holding, as the description says. The Black Sludge / Big Nugget item-replacement clauses have no equivalent item in this engine and are not ported.
- **Infatuation now works the ER way.** Vanilla gave an infatuated Pokemon a 50% chance to be immobilized each turn; in Elite Redux infatuation never stops you from acting - instead it cuts the infatuated Pokemon's Attack AND Sp. Atk in half for as long as it lasts, exactly as every ER ability that infatuates (Cute Charm, Pure Love, Yuki Onna, Entrance...) describes.
- **Black Shinies now grant Luck 5.** As the rarest shiny tier they were meant to beat the regular shiny cap of 3, but they were reading their ordinary variant luck instead. The value is computed on the fly from the black shiny flag, so existing saves pick it up automatically - nothing about your save data changes.
- **Stale-cache reports should be over.** Three layers were hardened: the link between the game and its art/audio library is now re-pinned automatically on every deploy (it used to need a manual update, and a forgotten one meant players kept old sprites or missing audio for days - it WAS out of date when we checked), the game code bundle now caches correctly instead of by browser guesswork, and a missing internal file that 404'd on every single game load is gone. Every bug-report log now also records exactly which build it came from, so a report from an outdated tab is recognizable instantly.
- **Game speed no longer resets behind your back.** The speed-option rework shipped with a settings migration that never stamped itself as done, so it re-ran on every game load AND on every in-game reset (retrying a battle, returning to the menu after a loss) - re-clamping whatever speed you had just picked back to 3x. It now runs once and stays done, and players whose old speed value was out of range for the new option list get it remapped instead of silently broken.
- **Locked innates no longer leak into battle.** A whole family of ER ability effects (type-chart rewrites like Overwhelm's "Dragon hits Fairies", field auras, crit boosts, attack-stat substitutes, protect bypasses and more) was read straight off the Pokemon's innate slots without checking whether you had actually unlocked them - so a LOCKED Overwhelm let your Zekrom hit Fairies with Dragon moves. All of these now respect the same unlock rules as every other ability: candy unlocks for your Pokemon, the level ramp for enemies.
- **Wildbolt Storm verified: it cannot miss in rain.** A report said it always misses in rain; the engine check shows the opposite - it keeps its never-miss-in-rain property, sets rain itself on use, and is 90% accurate outside rain. There is a dev scenario to confirm in-game; if you still see a rain miss, send logs from that scenario.
- **Wrong-sprite self-healing.** The rare "base Pokemon showing Mega artwork" display bug now heals itself: when the game detects a sprite texture bound to the wrong source, it evicts and reloads it on the spot, and logs a trace so we can hunt the root cause from tester logs.
- **New challenge: Mono Color (5 Favour).** Beat the run using only Pokemon of one dex color - Red, Green, Blue, White, Brown, Yellow, Purple, Pink, Gray or Black, straight from the ROM's own color table (Elite Redux customs included). Works like Mono Type: starters must match the color, and an evolution that changes color makes that Pokemon unusable, so plan your lines.
- **Difficulty rewards are split: low difficulties pay CANDY, high ones pay SHINIES.** Youngster grants DOUBLE candy gain, Ace 1.5x; Elite keeps its 1.5x wild shiny rate and Hell its 2x - the higher difficulties stay the shiny hunting grounds. The mode descriptions on the difficulty picker say so.
- **Ghost battles actually play their theme now.** "The Piano Before Cynthia" only played on the Gen 5 music preference; the default setting routed around it. It plays for everyone now.
- **Berry Smash feeds the right Pokemon now.** The user's berry was consumed but its effect landed on the TARGET (a reverse Pluck - one report had an enemy Gogoat feeding its berry to the player's Empoleon). The user now eats its own berry, as the move says.
- **Doubles Only freeze fixed.** Trainers whose lineup rolled a single Pokémon froze the forced double battle on the empty second slot; they now always field at least two.
- **Double-KO hard freeze fixed.** Knocking out BOTH enemy Pokemon in the same turn of a trainer double battle froze the game when the trainer had only one reserve left - the second replacement switch crashed mid-phase. It now resolves cleanly. This was the "have to hard reset, can't double KO" report.

## Abilities
- **Minion Control no longer hits 6 times at full power.** Per the ROM's own text, every strike past the first deals 10% damage - a full healthy party totals about 150% instead of 600%. Each hit still rolls secondary effects independently.
- **Aftermath no longer re-triggers on every hit of a multi-hit move.** A multi-hit KO used to leave the holder sitting at 1 HP through the whole volley with the ability popping on each strike. Now the KO strike ends the volley on the spot, Aftermath triggers once, and the holder immediately explodes - matching the ROM, where the rest of the volley never happens.
- **Justified now ABSORBS Dark-type moves.** Per the ER dex it "boosts Attack instead of being hit" - the holder takes no damage from Dark moves and gains +1 Attack, like Sap Sipper for Grass. It previously took the hit and then boosted.
- **Wishmaker (Dragonite Y and friends) now heals the RIGHT Pokemon.** The on-entry Wish used to land on the OPPONENT's slot, gifting the enemy the delayed heal. The Wish, and a batch of other self-targeted entry casts that had the same wiring (Heal Bell, Mist, Defense Curl, Magnet Rise, No Retreat, Dragon Cheer), now correctly target the holder's own side.
- **Two Step's dance follow-up no longer SELF-HITS after self-buff dances.** Using Quiver Dance or Victory Dance made the scripted Revelation Dance (and Blade Dance's Leaf Blade, Backflip's rider) strike the dancer itself, because those dances target the user. The follow-up now always aims at a real opponent.

## Eggs & Collection
- **The egg pool got a massive declutter: 161 imported duplicate forms no longer hatch.** All the separate Unown letters, Arceus type plates, Silvally memories, Pikachu caps, Furfrou trims, Rotom appliances, Genesect drives, Deoxys formes, Therian formes, Ogerpon masks, Paldean Tauros breeds, Calyrex riders, weather Castforms and other cosmetics are gone from eggs and starter select - the vanilla Pokemon already covers every one of those forms (via its own forms or form-change items like the Reveal Glass and the plates). Battle-only forms that should never have hatched (Busted Mimikyu, Gulping Cramorant, Sunshine Cherrim, Zygarde Complete) are gone too.
- **Nothing you own is lost.** If you had any removed form, its shiny (including the red and BLACK tiers), dex progress and candies are automatically carried over to the vanilla base Pokemon the next time the game loads. A red-shiny Unown letter becomes a red-shiny Unown. Existing saves, runs and parties are completely untouched.
- **The RDX tab now holds ALL Elite Redux customs.** The convergent and wholly-new species (Wispywaspy, the Iron paradox series, Heracreus, ...) used to hide in the Gen 9 column; they now live under RDX in both starter select and the Pokedex.
- **Mono-gen challenge: RDX is a pickable generation.** Run a challenge with only Elite Redux customs.
- **New 4th egg gacha machine: Redux Up!** Pulls from it heavily favor Elite Redux customs. It sits next to the other three machines, and the egg auto-restock setting can target it.

- **Redux catches land in the RDX slot now, not on the vanilla Pokemon.** Catching a Redux-form mon used to claim the VANILLA species' gen slot (a caught Spearow Redux took over gen 1 Spearow's spot, candy and cost included) while the real RDX entry stayed empty. New catches register the RDX entry directly, and on your next load any already-hijacked unlock moves itself over: the vanilla slot reverts to uncaught (unless you genuinely caught the vanilla form too) and the shiny tiers plus candies land on the RDX entry. Nothing is lost.

- **Redux forms use their OWN learnsets now.** A Redux-form mon used to read the vanilla species' level-up moves (Beedrill Redux never learned Icicle Spear). Every Redux form now mirrors its RDX species' real kit.

- **Every hatchable RDX Pokemon now has a full egg-move set.** Flygon Redux B, Mimikyu Apex, Ribombee Redux, Serena-Delphox, Weavile Redux and a few off-pool lines were missing theirs entirely - each got 4 kit-appropriate egg moves (e.g. Mimikyu Apex gets Dragon Ascent as its rare, Ribombee Redux gets Tail Glow). A CI gate now fails if any egg-pool custom ever ships without one.

## UI & Quality of Life
- **Save and Quit always pushes your full save to the cloud, and now TELLS you if that fails.** The push itself bypasses the usual sync throttling, so quitting is a manual sync button. New: when the cloud push does not go through (server hiccup, offline), you get a clear warning that the save is only on this device for now and will retry next session - no more silently quitting and assuming it synced.
- **Two new game-speed tiers: Hyper (7x) and Ludicrous (10x).** Pick them in Settings under Game Speed, or cycle with the in-battle speed hotkeys (which now reach the new tiers). Only animations and timers speed up - music and sound stay at normal pitch.
- **Reuse Last Setup is a real button now.** On the challenge screen, focus the bottom bar (reachable even with nothing selected if you have a saved setup) and press LEFT/RIGHT to switch it between Begin and "Reuse Last Setup". The R hotkey still works from the list.
- **Recoil moves say so.** Custom moves that mechanically cause recoil but whose description omitted it (like Shadow Hammer) now state the recoil percentage automatically.

## Pokemon
- **Redux and ER-custom black shinies show their REAL black art now.** Anyone who found a black shiny of a Redux form (or an ER custom) saw a dark-tinted normal sprite instead of the generated black smoke-halo art - the game looked the black atlas up under the shiny path, which never matched. The real sprites load from the first frame now, front and back.
- **Minion Control extra hits corrected to 10%** (was briefly 25% on staging) after checking the ROM's own long description.
- **Evolution screen shows the right Pokemon again.** For some shiny Redux lines (reported with Ralts Redux to Kirlia Redux) the cutscene and the congratulations screen kept showing the PRE-evolution because the evolved form's animation failed to build. The animation is now rebuilt on the spot.
- **No more early "Pokemon gods" on Youngster and Ace.** Two holes let 600+ BST monsters slip past the early-game gate before wave 55: the reroll safeguard gave up after 10 tries and kept the offender (guaranteed in biomes whose boss pool is all heavy hitters), and the level-based evolution step ran after the gate, evolving a harmless spawn straight into an endgame final form. Both are closed - the gate now re-checks evolved forms and falls back to a common-pool pick instead of surrendering.
- **No more early wild Shedinja.** Wonder Guard hard-walled early teams and ended runs; wild Shedinja is rerolled before wave 55 on every difficulty.
- **No more surprise permanent Megas from Weird Dream or GTS trades (the wave-13 Mega Urshifu).** Elite Redux registers each mega and primal form as its own internal species record, and the Weird Dream transformation and Global Trade System pools picked from ALL species by stat total - so a transformation or trade could hand you a permanent, unrevertable Mega (Mega Urshifu at wave 13 on Ace was this). Battle-only and mega records are now excluded from both pools everywhere, and on Youngster and Ace (pure vanilla modes) the pools contain no Elite Redux customs at all.
- **No more wild Redux forms on Youngster and Ace.** Wild spawns had a 1 in 8 chance to appear as their Elite Redux form on every difficulty, so the pure vanilla modes met ER customs (custom abilities, kits and types) in the wild. Redux forms now only spawn wild on Elite and Hell.
- **Roaming Gimmighoul evolves now** - plain level 50 into Gholdengo (the coin-counting requirement never triggered for the roaming form).
- **Every starter is guaranteed at least one damaging move.** Some lines (like Krabby Redux) open their learnset with only status moves and started the run unable to deal damage; the earliest damaging level-up move is slotted in automatically.
